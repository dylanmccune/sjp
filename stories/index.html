“As a kid, I wanted to be a paleontologist. Then love unearthed me from the streets.”

Shared by Mohamed

When I was a child, I loved digging holes and being outside in the dirt. This interest came from a group of paleontologists that visited my school. Life took a turn, and I found myself on the streets. But love and support unearthed me from that place, giving me hope for the future.

“San Francisco is a mess. I pick up 200 dirty heroin needles a day.”

Shared by Gedion

I’m not the type of person who particularly desires attention. I am not here to claim the face of homelessness, but I do what I can to keep my city clean, picking up hundreds of needles every day. It’s a small way to help my community.

Shared by Anita

Ohio is where I come from. And where alcoholism came, too. I’ve been out here in San Francisco for over 20 years now. This city has been both a challenge and a home, and I keep going, one day at a time.

“I fought self-destruction with creation.”

Shared by Ronnie Goodman

I grew up in San Francisco, in the Fillmore area. I’ve been in and out of prison most of my life. Last time, it was eight and a half years. Art and running helped me fight self-destruction and find a new path.
